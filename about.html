NOTEABLE FACTS


  • Al's Breakfast is reportedly the narrowest restaurant in the city of Minneapolis, at a width of ten feet (3.0m).

  • The restaurant's 14 stools have seated generations of local students, along with writer James Lileks and humorist Garrison Keillor, all of whom consider the tiny diner to be a state icon.

  • In 2004, Al's Breakfast won a James Beard Foundation award in the "America's Classics Restaurants" category.

  • Al's Breakfast appeared on the Food Network's Diners, Drive-Ins and Dives, in the episode "American Cookin'" in 2007.

AL'S STORY


  • Al's Breakfast was started by Al Bergstrom in 1950 and is currently owned and operated by Doug Grina and Alison Kirwin.

  • Al's serves made-from-scratch breakfast fare at a 14 seat counter in the Dinkytown neighborhood of Minneapolis, Minnesota.

  • We don't have a freezer, deep fryer, or microwave oven because we believe that meals taste best when they're made from real ingredients by people who care. That, and we don't have space for anything else.

BEFORE YOU GO


  • Payment: Al's accepts cash and personal checks. No credit cards.

  • The Line: Al's has 14 stools at a counter. Because we have so few seats, it is easy for a line to accumulate. Typically the line moves pretty quickly.

  • Closing Time: We close at 1pm every day that we're open. If you're in line at 1pm, we'll feed you. If you try to join the line at 1:01pm we'll inform you that we're closed.

  • Al's Breakfast appeared on the Food Network's Diners, Drive-Ins and Dives, in the episode "American Cookin'" in 2007.
